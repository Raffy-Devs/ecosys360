Forest Ecosystems

Forests are one of the most vital ecosystems on Earth, covering about 31% of the planet's land area. They are home to more than 80% of terrestrial species and are critical in regulating Earth's climate, water cycle, and carbon storage.

Types of Forests

Discover the three major forest types that shape our planet's terrestrial ecosystems, each with unique characteristics and vital ecological roles.

Tropical Forests

  • Found near the equator (Amazon, Congo Basin, Southeast Asia)
  • Contain over half of the world's plant and animal species
  • Store vast amounts of carbon ‚Äî one hectare can hold over 200 metric tons
  • Year-round warm temperatures and high rainfall
  • Multi-layered canopy structure supporting incredible biodiversity

Temperate Forests

  • Located in North America, Europe, and parts of Asia
  • Characterized by four seasons and deciduous trees
  • Key to supporting migratory bird species and diverse mammals
  • Rich soil layers from seasonal leaf fall
  • Moderate climate with distinct seasonal changes

Boreal (Taiga) Forests

  • Spread across Canada, Russia, and Scandinavia
  • The world's largest terrestrial biome
  • Store about 703 gigatonnes of carbon in biomass and soil
  • Dominated by coniferous trees adapted to cold climates
  • Critical habitat for many northern wildlife species
