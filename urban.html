Habitat Fragmentation

Urban development breaks up natural habitats, creating isolated patches that threaten wildlife survival.

  • Disrupted wildlife corridors
  • Reduced genetic diversity
  • Increased species isolation
  • Barriers to natural migration

Water Systems Impact

Urbanization dramatically alters natural water cycles and quality.

  • Increased runoff and flooding
  • Water pollution from urban waste
  • Groundwater depletion
  • Altered river systems

Ecosystem Services Loss

Natural processes that support life are disrupted by urban development.

  • Reduced carbon sequestration
  • Decreased air purification
  • Loss of pollination services
  • Diminished soil fertility
